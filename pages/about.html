Hey! I'm a 17 year old high school student from Lexington, Kentucky, who's interested in all things computer science. I've been coding since I was 9, when I would make text-based console games in Java, but since then I've broadened my horizons. I've tried everything from training a neural network to play asteroids, to competitive programming, to making this website. I've worked in Java, Python, C++, PHP, Swift, and now HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. (Sorry to flex)

I made this website for two reasons. The first is so I could try out web development by making a static website. The second is because of the lack of accessible information out there on these topics. Many of these algorithms are black boxes to most people; all you have to do is call a method and you're done. I decided it would be interesting to look into how these algorithms work behind the hood and explain it in easily understandable language and visuals, rather than a jumble of pseudocode and mathematical models. I hope you like it!

If you liked this site, consider checking out my Patreon, and if you have any suggestions or corrections, feel free to shoot me an email at 21lutzs@sayreschool.org. You can also check out my Github if you want. Have fun!
